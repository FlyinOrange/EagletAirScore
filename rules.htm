The Skyhigh Cup is a free cross-country competition for members of the Skyhigh Paragliding Club:
  • Who - Any club pilot who is intermediate or above.
  • Where - anywhere in Victoria except NEVHGC sites around Bright (including Mystic, Tawonga, Buffalo, Emu and the Pines).
  • When - Any weekend between the 1st of Oct to the 31st of March.
Registration. If you are intending to compete you must inform other pilots on Topica the Friday before the weekend. Or phone someone who will post your intentions to Topica. Other pilots must be given the opportunity to fly in the same conditions! You may not fly alone. Scoring http://skyhighparagliding.org/skyhighcup.htm Submitted tracklogs will be scored using an OLC sytle scoring system. Each tracklog with be optimised to include start, 3 waypoints and end points. Tracks where the landing point is near the starting point may gain bonus points based upon the area they enclose providing the track form a simple polygon. Your track will be viewable on the web, and you will be able to view other participants tracks. Team flying - To encourage team flying should there be a bonus points if pilots complete some of the flight with other pilots. On slow days I suspect this could be very useful. On faster days gun pilots may have to juggle gaggle points or loner speed. Downloads must be within two weeks of the flight or at a club meeting if you don't have your own cable. At the moment only .IGC files are accepted for scoring. Safety Stuff. Pilots must have a workable UHF. Any pilot tracklog submitted by a pilot flying without a radio will be considered to be invalid. Safe flying condition. If more than 50% of pilots present at a site see the conditions as un-flyable then the day will be considered to be invalid. If there is a valid day, pilots do not have to launch if they judge it invalid. If a pilot judges it valid and does not launch, there vote is consider to be for an invalid day. We encourage pilots at a site to fill out a running sheet on the day with mobile phone numbers and appoint a duty pilotas per the HGFA handbook.
